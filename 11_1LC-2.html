Encounter Journal

  • Can we still believe the Bible?

  • How do we decide?

Share

We cannot use contemporary norms of knowledge as the foundation for our belief. It’s popular to see the Bible as just another history book or as a “wisdom” book that is no different from other literature. It’s also popular to read the Bible through the lens of our culture and to judge what happened in biblical times based on what is happening in our world today. (For example, I don’t see miracles happening around me, so they mustn’t have happened in Jesus’ day.) Building truth on a human foundation places one’s own knowledge and wisdom above that of God’s Word, elevating self above God—which was the sin of Israel and Judah. Christ also met this same temptation in the wilderness, to build on human foundation, but instead responded with, “It is written . . .” Human knowledge should never be the foundation of our belief.

In this unit we will be exploring:

  • The trustworthiness of the Bible (its relevance to us today)

  • The Bibles endurance through history
