Christian History

The First Christians, ad 1–100

  • Persecution after the fire in Rome (Nero)
  • Division after the fall of Jerusalem (Vespasian, Titus)
  • Emperors who claimed to be gods in their lifetime (Domitian)

Defending the Truth, ad 100–300

  • Deepening persecution of Christians (Ignatius, Polycarp, and Blandina martyred)
  • False teachings come from within the Christian church (It was a struggle for local churches to maintain the truth about Jesus when they were being aggressively persecuted.)

Persecuted to Preferred, ad 300–500

  • How Roman persecution of Christians ended (Emperor Diocletian, Emperor Galerius, Constantine)
  • Arius’s teachings that Jesus was less than God and the Council of Nicaea

Christianity on the Move, ad 300–500

  • Desert monks (Gregory of Nazianzus, Apollinaris, Council of Constantinople)
  • Roman Empire fell to barbarians
  • Councils of Ephesus and Chalcedon
  • Monks and nuns preserve learning

A Church Divided, ad 500–1300.

  • Controversy around icons
  • The Franks (Charlemagne, Pope Leo III, Alcuin of York)
  • Reasons why the church split
  • The papal bull
  • Crusade against Muslims, Peter the Hermit, and the Western Crusaders in the Fourth Crusade
  • Missionaries (Boniface and Lioba, brothers Cyril and Methodius)
  • Reformers (Berno, Bernard of Clairvaux)
  • Mendicants (Peter Waldo, Francis of Assisi)
  • Dominicans (Thomas Aquinas)

Note: This Christian History Made Easy curriculum is from an ecumenical perspective, which means that the great controversy theme is not inherent.
