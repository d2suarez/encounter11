WHO WROTE THE BIBLE?

3 For use in Animated/Engaged Learning.

Activity source: Rachel Billmeyer, Strategies to Engage the Mind of the Learner (Hawker Brownlow Education, 2010), p. 60

Mark your initial responses as to whether you think these statements are true or false from what you currently know of the Bible.

  1. The Bible was written over a span of 1,000 years or more.
  2. There are 100 million new copies of the Bible printed each year.
  3. We must turn to the text itself to learn how and why the Bible came into being, rather than to scholars.
  4. The Pentateuch is the first six books of the Bible, written by Moses.
  5. Moses received the holy words from God and wrote the Pentateuch while in the wilderness and passed them on to the people.
  6. Jews believe that Moses wrote the Torah (first five books of the Bible), but God gave him some of the ideas in the books.
  7. God chose human beings to write the Bible because His message is for man.
  8. The Old Testament includes the life and teachings of Jesus, Gospels, acts of apostles, letters, Revelation. The New Testament includes the Law, history books (prophets), and other writings.
  9. The canonized books were those that had evidence of old manuscripts.
