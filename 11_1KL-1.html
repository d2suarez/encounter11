DRAMA SCRIPT:

BEANS

8 For use in Kaizen Learning.

By Kendra Perry

CAST:

  • Maureen

  • Felicia

  • Bill

  • Joe

  • Mail-Guy...er, Person

SETTING:

The trenches of Spiritual warfare

Note: We often do this script with different genders in the various roles. With a bit of name changing, for example, Joe becomes Joy, it seems to work well.

(Maureen, Felicia, Bill, and Joe are huddled around a small portable stove. A pot of beans is cooking on it. Everyone looks bored and depressed. For a long moment, no one says a word. Finally . . .)

Bill: Hey, those beans must be almost done.

(Joe gets up and stirs them, tries some, shakes his head. Sits down. Silence. Finally, Maureen can’t stand it anymore.)

Maureen: (Jumping up) I can’t STAND it anymore! Is it always this boring?

This is a war zone! There has to be something more interesting than beans cooking!

(Everyone looks at her flatly. Embarrassed, she sits down again. Silence.)

Bill: How about those beans?

(Joe gets up and stirs them, tries some, nods, dishes some out, and passes around bowls. Finally, he sits down. Everybody eats in silence. Suddenly from offstage, a huge explosion and gunfire. Everybody looks around. Mail-Guy comes crawling on stage, ducking bullets, etc. He is dragging his mail bag behind him.)

Joe: Hey, it’s the mail guy! Mail-Guy: Mail person.

(There is a flurry of activity; everyone wants to help the mail get through. They drag Mail-Guy into the circle, talking excitedly. Everyone is crowding around, poking, prodding, and trying to get their mail.)

Bill: Anything for me?

Joe: How about me?

Mail-Guy: OK, now, everybody just sit down and take it easy! Let me catch my breath here!

(All sit down but are on the edges of their seats, unable to restrain their enthusiasm)

Mail-Guy: Boy, I almost thought I wasn’t going to make it this time. It’s rough out there! (Nods of agreement. Mail-Guy pulls a packet of letters out of his bag.) OK . . . Joe, here you go. (Hands Joe two thin envelopes.) Bill . . . (Hands Bill one thin envelope.) Oh, and here you go, Felicia. Be careful with that one—it’s heavy! (Hands Felicia a huge envelope decorated with tender care. Maureen looks on expectantly.) Well, that’s it. (To Maureen) Sorry, bud. Nothing for you again today, either. (Maureen looks crestfallen.) Well, I’ve got to be moving. So long! (Everyone waves goodbye as Mail-Guy exits under heavy fire. All begin tearing into their mail except Felicia, who merely turns her envelope over again and again, examining it.)

Maureen: (eagerly) Well, what did you get? Bill?

Bill: A letter from my best friend back home . . . Ed McMahon!

It says here I may have won a million dollars!

Maureen:(Shakes head, turns to Joe. Felicia is watching the exchange with interest, letter in hand.) What you got there, Joe?

Joe: Man, just the same old thing every time. I’m preapproved for a full line of credit, blah blah blah (tosses envelope over shoulder). And I don’t want to become a member of the National Geographic Society. Doesn’t even have my name on the letter (tosses it over shoulder, dejected).

Maureen: (also saddened) That’s too bad, Joe. What did you get Felicia? Looked like an awfully full envelope!

Felicia: (proudly holds out letter for all to admire) A letter from Jay, my one true love back home. Look how he decorated the envelope and everything! (Hands it to Maureen, who admires Jay’s handiwork and passes the letter on around the circle.)

Maureen: Hey, that’s really something! You’re a lucky girl. Wow. Someone at home who loves you and writes you letters.

Felicia: Yep. Jay writes me all the time. (Joe and Bill nod in agreement.) I get at least one envelope every time the Mail Guy comes around. (Takes envelope back and puts it in her pants pocket. Mail-Guy can shout “Mail Person!” from offstage at this point.)

Maureen: (stunned) Wait! Aren’t you going to open it?

Felicia: (laughs) Nah.

Maureen: But don’t you want to know what it says?

Felicia: (matter of factly) I do know what it says. I love you, I miss you, I hope you’re safe. Stuff like that. You know, your basic love letter.

Bill: Yeah, Felicia never opens her letters from Jay. She must have close to a hundred of them in her backpack.

Felicia: Now where did those beans get to?

(Everyone but Maureen turns back to their beans. Bill is about to take another bite of beans when he glances up. Everyone suddenly goes into slow motion.)

Bill: (points to the sky stage right) INCOMING!!! (At the warning, everyone, still in slow motion, dives for cover. During the confusion, Maureen makes an elaborate grab for the envelope tucked in Felicia’s back pocket and swipes it. After a moment, everyone returns to normal speed, reacting to the horrible shelling.)

Felicia: Wow! That was a close one!

Bill: Yeah! Remember last Tuesday?

Joe: Phew!

(Everyone freezes but Maureen, who steps forward and pulls out the stolen letter.)

Maureen: (Reading)

Dear Felicia,

I can’t understand why you’re not responding to my letters! Has something happened to you? Are you hurt? I miss you so much and long to know what is happening in your life. If I don’t hear from you soon, I fear I may abandon hope of ever seeing you again! I think of you every minute of every day.

(Maureen looks up) Wow! This is good stuff! I wish I had someone who would write me like this . . . Hey! (Looks around, a sly grin coming over her face. Places letter in her own pocket, takes out pen and paper, and begins writing.)

Dear Jay, you don’t know me, but I feel like I’ve known you forever . . .

(Freeze)
