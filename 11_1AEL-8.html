The Bible claims that it is God’s inspired true word. It tells you that it will change your life, making clear the darkness in front of you. You may have experienced this in your life, and some of you may not have.

Jeremiah 15:16:

“Your words were found, and I ate them,

And Your word was to me the joy and rejoicing of my heart;

For I am called by Your name.” (NKJV)

Pair-share your thoughts on the following questions

  • Do you read your Bible?
  • If so, what do you read? How do you read it?
  • Do you think there is a difference between reading your Bible and studying it? (If so, what?)
  • Do you ever read the Bible with your boyfriend or girlfriend or with family? If so, what does that look like? (What do you read? How do you study or explore it?)
  • What difference do you think reading the Bible could make to your life and the relationships that are important to you?

Sometimes it’s hard to know where and how to start reading the Bible. You might have grown up going to church and sat through Bible classes, and still you might not really know how to study it for yourself. There is a difference between reading and studying the Bible, and there are definitely ways you can bring the Bible into your everyday life and relationships.
