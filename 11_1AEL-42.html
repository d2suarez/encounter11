There were also people in the Bible who suffered and were persecuted because of their faith in God. History tells us that Peter was crucified upside-down and that Paul was beheaded by Nero; Steven was stoned (Acts 7:54–60); James was killed with a sword (Acts 12:1–5); John the Baptist was beheaded (Matt. 14:10–12.)

A glance at Christian history shows centuries of persecution from the Jews and later the Romans. For the Christian believer, persecution was a way of life and in many cases the best form of evangelism (1 Pet. 3:15–17; 4:12–19). Eventually, persecution eased when Christians were placed in prominent offices or positions of influence. It does seem odd that the next form of persecution would come out the Church itself.

The inspired Word of God is so precious; therefore, Satan has tried to attack it in so many different ways throughout history.

Note: There were many pagans and heretics of other descriptions who were also persecuted once Christianity gained power. Some Christians were obsessed with martyrdom and worshiped martyrs.
